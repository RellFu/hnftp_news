A cultural and tourism delegation from South China's Hainan province visited Moscow and St Petersburg from Feb 21 to 25, 2026, to promote tourism and deepen cultural exchanges with Russia at the invitation of the Moscow City Tourism Committee.
Actors from Hainan gave performance in Russia, Feb 25, 2026. [Photo provided to en.hainan.gov.cn]
Organized by the Department of Tourism, Culture, Radio, Television and Sports of Hainan Province, the delegation included more than 10 performers from the Hainan Provincial Ethnic Song and Dance Ensemble. They participated in the 2026 Moscow Spring Festival celebrations, a major cultural event hosted by the Moscow City Government and supported by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the Russian Federation and the Chinese Embassy in Russia.
Russian spectators gathered to watch the performance, Feb 25, 2026. [Photo provided to en.hainan.gov.cn]
Performances were staged at Manezhnaya Square near Red Square, where the Hainan artists presented nine shows over three days, highlighting the province's ethnic traditions and tropical allure.
The repertoire included the opening dance Please Come to Tianya Haijiao, the nationally listed intangible cultural heritage performance Auspicious Bamboo and Wood, and creative adaptations of Russian classics using Li ethnic musical instruments, including Moscow Nights, The Cranberries Are Blooming, and Katyusha. The traditional Li Bamboo Pole Dance was also performed, showcasing Hainan's unique cultural heritage.
The performances attracted large audiences and warm responses, enhancing the visibility of Hainan's tourism and culture among Russian visitors.
Performers from China and Russia posed for a group photo, Feb 25, 2026. [Photo provided to en.hainan.gov.cn]
The visit strengthened cultural and tourism ties between Hainan and Russia and helped build a platform for future cooperation. Hainan will continue to expand international cultural exchanges and promote tourism collaboration to support the development of the Hainan Free Trade Port.
