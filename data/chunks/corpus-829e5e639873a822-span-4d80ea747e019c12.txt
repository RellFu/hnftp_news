Underdeveloped coastal frontier transforms into a top destination for local island life. In June 1097, renowned poet Su Shi of the Northern Song Dynasty (960-1127) landed on Hainan, China's second largest island, and was forced into exile. The 60-year-old set out from Huizhou, Guangdong province, the destination of his previous banishment. He stayed on the island for three years, traveling from one place to another, composing poems, and participating in local development. For example, he discovered spots to dig for and collect reliable underground water, guided locals who were primarily fishermen to properly cultivate farmland, and helped open schools.
