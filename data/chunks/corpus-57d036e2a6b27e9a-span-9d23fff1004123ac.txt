Preparing this high-quality meat is a simple process: blanching, stir-frying, and slow stewing, seasoned with just salt and white pepper. The result is a milky-white broth and tender, lean meat. Hainan province boasts a rich culinary scene for foodies, featuring specialties such as Hele crabs, Dongshan goat, Wenchang chicken, and zaopocu hotpot. [Photo provided to China Daily] Perhaps the most famous dish associated with the island is Hainanese chicken rice, which has become a staple across Southeast Asia. It is believed to have been introduced by Chinese immigrants in the late 19th and early 20th centuries, who blended their own culinary traditions with local flavors of Southeast Asia.
