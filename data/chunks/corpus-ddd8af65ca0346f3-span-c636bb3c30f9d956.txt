The number of visitors flying to Sanya on non-Chinese passports increased more than fourfold, with arrivals from Australia and Spain each rising more than fourfold, according to travel platform Qunar. International arrivals in Haikou by air doubled. The growth reflects Hainan's expanded visa-free policies, part of China's broader push to develop the island into a globally connected free trade port. The measures include visa-free entry for nationals of 86 countries, as well as special arrangements such as a 144-hour visa-free entry for foreign tour groups from Hong Kong and Macao and a 15-day visa-free entry for cruise tour groups.
