[Photo provided to China Daily] Many shun the usual choice of Sanya, a popular Hainan destination, due to its touristy hustle and bustle. Some travel to Haikou, the slightly overshadowed capital of the province, because it is quieter. It also offers a chance to immerse oneself in the daily routines of the locals. To become an authentic "islander", visitors begin their mornings with a sip of tea and close the day with a bowl of refreshing qingbuliang, a sweet soup found at the night market. Taking a train trip of one to two hours southward to Wanning, travelers can savor the enticing aroma of coffee beans produced in Xinglong town.
