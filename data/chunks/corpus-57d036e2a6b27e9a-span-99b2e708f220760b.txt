Hainan province boasts a rich culinary scene for foodies, featuring specialties such as Hele crabs, Dongshan goat, Wenchang chicken, and zaopocu hotpot. [Photo provided to China Daily] The teahouse heritage is believed to have emerged in the late 19th century, when large groups of Chinese immigrants went to Southeast Asia to make a living. When they returned home, they brought back the concept of coffee drinking and cafeterias, which blended with local tea culture. The leisurely lifestyle associated with these teahouses was named after the old men who spent much of their time there, embodying a relaxed and unhurried way of life.
