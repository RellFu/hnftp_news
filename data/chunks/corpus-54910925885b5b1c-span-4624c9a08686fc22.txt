An aerial view of Haikou Volcanic Cluster Global Geopark. [Photo/VCG] Haikou's livability is rooted in its exceptional natural environment, beginning with air quality that ranks among the best in China. Statistics indicate that multiple indicators, including PM10, have met the country's highest air quality standards for seven years in a row. The city has pioneered an innovative wetland ecosystem management approach, expanding its total wetland area to 32,202. 38 hectares — 13. 32 percent of its total land area. This achievement contributed to Haikou being designated an International Wetland City in 2018. A view of sunset glow over Haikou Bay.
